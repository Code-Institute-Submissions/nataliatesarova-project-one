Laser Eye Surgery


Laser eye surgery corrects short-sightedness (myopia), long-sightedness (hypermetropia), astigmatism and problems with reading vision (presbyopia). There are variety of different laser techniques to correct each condition by reshaping the cornea which adjusts the focus power of the eye.


LASIK: A flap is created in the cornea and the underlying tissue is reshaped with the laser.


LASEK: An alternative to LASIK for patients with thinner corneas. The procedure does not create a flap so reshaping occurs on the surface which requires a longer recovery time.


SMILE: The most technologically advanced type of laser eye surgery resulting in the highest level of accuracy and briefest recovery time.


Cataract and Refractive Lens Exchange Surgery


Femtosecond laser-assisted cataract and lens surgery is one of the latest advances in eye surgery. It is perfomed with laser instead of the traditional scalpel allowing unrivalled precision and outcomes.

Implantable Collamer Lens


The implantable collamer lens is placed within the eye rather than on the surface of the eye as in the case of contact lenses. It is especially useful for patients unsuitable for laser eye surgery.
